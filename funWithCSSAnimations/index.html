Animations vs Transitions


Fight! Fight! Fight!

Animations


Twists, twirls, transforms and tweens. 
Do that 4 times fast. 
In reverse. 
After waiting 1 second. 
And do it on a bezier curve.

Transitions

Get into that state
Wait 2 seconds before starting
Do it over 1.2 seconds
On a bezier curve

Wait, What?

Animations do stuff repeatedly
Transitions go from one state to another

Caveats

"auto"
Also the auto value is often a very complex case. The specification asks not to animate from and to such a value.

"display:none", appendChild()
Care should also be taken when using a transition immediately after adding the element to the DOM using .appendChild() or removing its display: none; property

https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/Guide/CSS/Using_CSS_transitions

To the keyframes!

Keyframes

Define a set of animation rules at particular times.

The most basic:

@keyframes bounce{
  from {
    top: 0px;
  }
  to {
    top: -10px;
  }
}
from and to are aliases to 0% and 100% respectively

KEYFRAMES

Working with percentages:

@keyframes bounce{
  0% {
    top: 0px;
  }
  25%{
    top: 2px;
  }
  50% {
    top: -5px;
  }
  100% {
    top: -10px;
  }
}

Feel the POWA!

Finally, finite control over what happens.

On your schedule:
  • Bounce around the page
  • Fade out
  • Change Color
  • Twist


All in one keyframe that you can re-use.

Animate

Lets get it moving already...

THE SYNTAX 

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
Let's break that down

Name

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
The name of the animation you are working with

Duration 

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
              || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
How long a single cycle of your animation should run 

examples:
  • 0s  (default)
  • 2s
  • 10s
  • 100ms

Timing Function

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
Define the easing or use a custom bezier curve.

examples:
  • ease  (default)
  • ease-in
  • linear
  • cubic-bezier(.17,.67,.83,.67)

Delay

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
How long to wait before starting to animate

examples:
  • 0s   (default)
  • 2s
  • 100ms

Iteration Count

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
How many times to run

examples:
  • infinite  (default)
  • 2
  • 1000

Direction

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
Should the animation play in reverse? 
Should we alternate?

examples:
  • normal (default)
  • reverse
  • alternate
  • alternate-reverse

Fill Mode

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
What styles should be applied before and after the animation? 
Defaulted to none at the cost of many headaches.

examples:
  • none (default)
  • forwards
  • backwards
  • both

Play State

animate: <name> || <duration> || <timing-function> || <delay> || <iteration-count>
          || <direction> || <fill-mode> || <play-state> [, ...];
Determine if the animation is running or paused.

examples:
  • paused
  • running  (default)
